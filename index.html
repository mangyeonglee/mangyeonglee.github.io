Mangyeong Lee is a Ph.D. candidate in the department of digital health at the Samsung Advanced Institute for Health Sciences and Technology (SAIHST), Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU), Korea. He also works at the center for clincial epidemiology in the Samsung Medical Center (SMC), Seoul, Korea. Currently he is advised by Juhee Cho and his research interests mainly focus on building a theory of mobile-specific health behavior which designs persuasive technologies and empowers patient engagement in the clinical environment. Especially, he is striving for demonstrating the clinical efficacy of mobile-based symptom management using patient-reported outcomes measures (PROMs) in cancer treatment.

/ Projects in Charge

JAMES

#funded_by_nrf #gpf #chatbot
JAMES (Just a AI-based MEasuring System) is a chatbot-based symptom monitoring service for cancer patients with active treatment.

ePROMs

#nrf_and_mfds #patient_reported_outcomes
This project aims to develop electronic patient-reporeted-outcome measures for empowering paitent-centeredness, facilitating shared decision making and patient-physician communication, and increasing qualtiy of life and treatment effect.

ACTION

#funded_by_nrf #mhealth_disparities #ncd
mHealth has been becoming one of the main options for managing chronic disease. However, there is still a vulnerable population such as the middle-aged and older. This study aims to identify the barriers to use mhealth app among the population.

Haily!

#funded_by_mist #pediatric_obesity #mhealth
Haily is a prototype service that provides customized food information and recipes that take into account the nutritional status of each child.

National Survey

#funded_by_mohw #precision_medicine
Precision Medicine (PM) is a clinical approach which takes account individual variability of each patient. This project is a nationwide survey to evaluate awareness, knowledge, attitudes, and willingness toward PM

TakeOut

#funded_by_motie #wearable_ix #ubicomp_16
TakeOut app provides distributed interface using the rotary wheel on a wearable in the digital-drawing environment. Through Imitating color blending or thickness control with the wheel, users can feel a more realistic experience.

SLOWEE

#funded_by_motie #physical_computing #chi_16 #ubicomp_16
Eating fast can have an influence on occurring digestive dysfunction, metabolic syndrome, and even esophageal cancer. This project aims to help people controlling their eating speed with lighting and vibrating guide. (demo)

Samsung EDGE Project

#funded_by_samsung #iot_home_appliance
This project aims to develop IoT scenarios for the futuristic refrigerator of Samsung Electronics.

CIRCLE ROUND

#funded_by_motie #cooperative_tool #cscw_16
project aims to develop a cooperative tool using two physical rotary dials which can assign the authorization of screen sharing and remote control. (demo)
